Description

The city of Malang is under siege by the army of City X for the next $Q$ days! Fortunately, Pak Dengklek is a spy from Malang. He scouted information that City X has $N$ soldiers, and the $i$-th soldier has strength $A_i$. Pak Dengklek wishes to rescue the city of Malang by blocking all the soldiers.

To block soldiers from City X, Pak Dengklek has built a series of $10^{100}$ magical portals at the border of Malang. All soldiers will always march together, passing through the portals one at a time. Every time all the soldiers pass through a portal, the portal will magically block them as follows: let there be $k$ soldiers in front of the portal, then all soldiers with strength exactly $k$ will not be able to pass the portal, and they have to retreat to their base in City X. All the remaining soldiers will march through the next portal, and so on.

Pak Dengklek is said to have rescued Malang on a day if no soldiers can pass through all the portals. However, notice that there may exist a configuration of $N$ soldiers, such that it is possible for one of them to pass through all the portals. Therefore, Pak Dengklek may use a magical flashlight that can change any soldier's strength to any value. The flashlight is energy-intensive, so Pak Dengklek wants to use the flashlight to change the strength of as few soldiers as possible. Pak Dengklek has to use the flashlight before the soldiers start to pass through the first portal. The changes by the flashlight are only temporary and will only last until the end of the day.

Apparently, City X is aware of Pak Dengklek's espionage, so City X will do the following strategy: at the beginning of the $j$-th day, the $X_j$-th soldier will change its strength to $Y_j$. The change is permanent and persists for the following days.

Pak Dengklek wants to know, for each day $j$, what is the minimum number of soldiers that must have their strengths changed by the flashlight so that Malang can be rescued on that day. Help Pak Dengklek!

Constraints

  • $1 \leq N, Q \leq 200\,000$
  • $1 \leq A_i \leq 1\,000\,000$ for $1 \leq i \leq N$
  • $1 \leq X_j \leq N$ for $1 \leq j \leq Q$
  • $1 \leq Y_j \leq 1\,000\,000$ for $1 \leq j \leq Q$

Subtasks

  1. (8 points) $N \leq 5; Q \leq 10$
  2. (6 points) $A_i$ and $Y_j$ are either $1$ or $N$
  3. (14 points) $N \leq 100; Q \leq 10$
  4. (13 points) $N \leq 1000; Q \leq 10$
  5. (17 points) $N \leq 1000; Q \leq 5000$
  6. (19 points) For each test case, there are at most $50$ unique soldiers' strengths at all times.
  7. (23 points) No additional constraints.

Input

The input is given in the following format:

N
A1 A2 ... AN
Q
X1 Y1
X2 Y2
X3 Y3
...
...
XQ YQ

Output

Output $Q$ lines, where the $j$-th line represents the minimum number of soldiers that must have their strengths changed by Pak Dengklek's magical flashlight on the $j$-th day.

Sample Input

4
1 1 1 2
5
3 2
1 4
4 3
3 4
4 4

Sample Output

2
1
0
1
0

Explanation

On the first day, the strengths of the soldiers are $[1, 1, 2, 2]$. One optimal way to rescue the city of Malang is to alter the strength of the first soldier to $4$, and the third soldier to $4$ too, thus their strengths become $[4, 1, 4, 2]$. All soldiers will then start to pass the portals, and the following happens.

  1. $A = [4, 1, 4, 2]$: $4$ remaining soldiers. The soldiers with strength $4$ can't pass the portal.
  2. $A = [1, 2]: $2$ remaining soldiers. The soldier with strength $2$ can't pass the portal.
  3. $A = [1]: $1$ remaining soldier. The soldier with strength $1$ can't pass the portal.
  4. No soldiers left and Pak Dengklek rescued the city of Malang.

On the second day, the strengths of the soldiers are $[4, 1, 2, 2]$. One optimal way to rescue the city of Malang is to alter the strength of the third soldier to $4$, thus their strengths become $[4, 1, 4, 2]$. All soldiers will then start to pass the portals, and the same things happen to the soldiers as the first day when they passed the portals.

On the third day, the strengths of soldiers are $[4, 1, 2, 3]$ Pak Dengklek does not need to alter any soldier's strength, because when the soldiers pass the portals, the following will happen.

  1. $A = [4, 1, 2, 3]$: $4$ remaining soldiers. The soldier with strength $4$ can't pass the portal.
  2. $A = [1, 2, 3]$: $3$ remaining soldiers. The soldier with strength $3$ can't pass the portal.
  3. $A = [1, 2]$: $2$ remaining soldiers. The soldier with strength $2$ can't pass the portal.
  4. $A = [1]$: $1$ remaining soldier. The soldier with strength $1$ can't pass the portal.
  5. No soldiers left and Pak Dengklek rescued the city of Malang.

On the fourth day, the strengths of the soldiers are $[4, 1, 4, 3]$. One optimal way to rescue the city of Malang is to alter the strength of the fourth soldier to $4$, thus their strengths become $[4, 1, 4, 4]$. All soldiers will then start to pass the portals, and the following happens.

  1. $A = [4, 1, 4, 4]$: $4$ remaining soldiers. The soldiers with strength $4$ can't pass the portal.
  2. $A = [1]$: $1$ remaining soldier. The soldier with strength $1$ can't pass the portal.
  3. No soldiers left and Pak Dengklek rescued the city of Malang.

On the fifth day, the strengths of the soldiers are $[4, 1, 4, 4]$. Pak Dengklek does not need to alter any soldier's strength, because when the soldiers pass the portals, the same will happen as the fourth day.
