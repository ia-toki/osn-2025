Description

For the upcoming vacation, Pak Dengklek decided to explore Jatim Park.

Jatim Park has $N$ attractions, numbered from $1$ to $N$, and these attractions are connected by $M$ footpaths, numbered from $1$ to $M$. Footpath $i$ connects attractions $U_i$ and $V_i$ bidirectionally, and requires $W_i$ seconds to walk through it. There are two types of footpath as described by $Z_i$ as follows.

  • If $Z_i = 1$, then this footpath is not sheltered and can only be passed when rain is not falling.
  • If $Z_i = 2$, then this footpath is sheltered and can always be passed, regardless of whether rain is falling or not.

In the midst of the rainy season, Pak Dengklek forgot to bring his umbrella, so Pak Dengklek are relying on $Q$ weather forecasts. Right now (at $0$-th second), the weather is clear. Weather forecast $j$ says that rain will start falling at the $T_j$-th second and will keep falling non-stop. To avoid being caught in the rain, Pak Dengklek can only walk through sheltered footpaths or stay at any attractions. In other words, Pak Dengklek must not be in the middle of an unsheltered footpath when the rain is falling.

Pak Dengklek is now at attraction $1$ and want to visit attraction $N$. Suppose Pak Dengklek follow weather forecast $j$, that is, the rain starts falling at the $T_j$-th second, count the shortest duration for Pak Dengklek to reach his destination without being caught in the rain, or report that the destination is impossible to reach.

Constraints

  • $2 \le N \le 100\,000$
  • $1 \le M \le 200\,000$
  • $1 \le U_i \lt V_i \le N$, for all $1 \le i \le M$
  • $1 \le W_i \le 10\,000$, for all $1 \le i \le M$
  • $Z_i = 1$ atau $Z_i = 2$, for all $1 \le i \le M$
  • $1 \le Q \le 100\,000$
  • $1 \le T_j \le 10^{9}$, for all $1 \le j \le Q$

Subtasks

  1. (7 points) $Z_i = 2$; $W_i = 1$
  2. (8 points) $Z_i = 2$
  3. (6 points) $Z_i = 1$
  4. (19 points) $N, M, Q \le 2000$
  5. (22 points) If $Z_i = 2$, then $V_i = N$.
  6. (19 points) $N, M \le 2000$
  7. (19 points) No additional constraints.

Input

The input is given in the following format: 

N M
U1 V1 W1 Z1
U2 V2 W2 Z2
⋮
UM VM WM ZM
Q
T1
T2
⋮
TQ

Output

Output $Q$ lines. The $j$-th line contains an integer representing the shortest travel duration from attraction $1$ to attraction $N$ if the rain starts falling at the $T_j$-th second. If attraction $N$ can not be reached, output -1.

Sample Input

4 4
1 3 20 1
2 3 30 2
2 3 20 1
2 4 10 2
3
100
20
1

Sample Output

50
60
-1

Explanation

The Jatim Park from the input can be illustrated as follows.

In the first weather forecast, $T_1 = 100$. Pak Dengklek can use footpaths $1$, $3$, then $4$, and reach the destination in $50$ seconds before the rain falls.

In the second weather forecast, $T_2 = 20$. Pak Dengklek can walk from attraction $1$ to attraction $2$ using the only footpath in $20$ seconds, right before the rain starts falling. Next, Pak Dengklek have to use sheltered footpaths, and reach the destination in a total time of $60$ seconds.

In the third weather forecast, $T_3 = 1$. The only footpath that Pak Dengklek can walk through requires $20$ seconds, and rain will already start falling by then, so it can not be used. Since Pak Dengklek can not reach his destination, you have to output -1.
